the mission demands.

This brief grew out of our conversation about mapping H&H's revenue streams and turns that thinking into a map we can build strategy from together.

Strategic Context

Where H&H stands - and what it requires.

Most nonprofits at this stage are running on one or two revenue streams. H&H has six. That's a genuine strength. It's also a signal that the org has outgrown an informal approach to revenue management. The opportunity isn't to add more streams - it's to understand what each one does and doesn't unlock, and sequence them toward a model that actually sustains the mission long-term.

The question this brief is designed to surface is not about money. It's about mission reach. How far can H&H go if José and Nate remain in their current operating position? And what becomes possible when they don't have to be? The answer to that question is what the whiteboard meeting is for.

Stream-by-Stream Analysis

What's working, what needs attention, and what needs a conversation.

Each stream has its own status, strategic logic, and set of open questions. Tap any stream to read the detail.

01
School and Program Contracts
TRUSD · NUSD ELOP · CCCA Sports Academy → SCUSD, EGUSD
Protect + Expand ▲

This is the load-bearing revenue - reliable, high-value, and repeatable. The TRUSD contract at $105K is the current anchor. This is the only money H&H fully controls: no application cycle, no funder approval, no restricted use. Growing this tier through SCUSD and EGUSD is the most direct path to unrestricted revenue. The strategic priority isn't just doing great work and hoping contracts renew - it's building a pipeline and expansion system so growth happens by design.

→What are the renewal timelines for each active contract and who owns each relationship?
→Is there a documented outreach pathway for SCUSD and EGUSD?
→Is there a path to a larger TRUSD contract or a staff development add-on before May 2026 expiration?
02
Trailblazers Club
Monthly recurring donors · Community sustainability engine
Build and Scale ▲

Monthly recurring giving is the most sustainable community revenue an org can build. It compounds over time, runs independent of grant cycles, and grows with storytelling and community connection. Trailblazers Club is the right vehicle - it likely punches below its weight right now not because of weak community loyalty, but because there's no formal stewardship structure yet. This stream needs a launch plan, a donor journey, and consistency before it can scale. See the scenario grid below for what it can produce.

→How many active Trailblazers currently? What is the average monthly gift?
→Is there a donor welcome sequence, impact update, and renewal touchpoint?
→Who owns donor communications and how often do they go out?
03
Grants and Institutional Funding
Golden 1 · Sutter · OJJDP · CNRA · Capacity-Building Pipeline
Shift the Mix ▲

H&H has an active grants pipeline. The critical shift is in what kinds of grants are being pursued. Program grants fund program delivery but cannot fund the org. Capacity-building grants and general operating support grants are the missing layer - they fund staff, infrastructure, leadership, and systems. Activating this grant category is the most direct route to closing the structural gap. The pipeline needs to shift from reactive (apply when you hear about it) to prospective (12-month calendar, pre-built outcome data, active funder relationships).

→Is there a 12-month grant calendar with submission and reporting dates?
→Are program outcomes tracked in a format that maps to what funders ask for?
→Which capacity-building funders are the right fit for H&H's stage and geography?
04
Brand Partnerships and Sponsorships
Auburn AleHouse · Old Trapper · Kühl · Branded Bills
Formalize ▼

H&H has active brand relationships with real visibility and community reach. Auburn AleHouse is the most developed - a co-branded beer with three more releases in progress and event partnerships that extend the mission into community spaces. Old Trapper, Kühl, and Branded Bills are active partners as well. The strategic question is what the actual revenue structure looks like for each. Are these producing cash revenue, in-kind product, or exposure? The answer determines how they fit into the sustainability strategy and whether the AleHouse model can be replicated.

Revenue Clarity Needed

For each active partnership: what is cash revenue, what is in-kind product, and what is exposure value? These are different things with different strategic implications. Cash closes the gap. In-kind reduces costs. Exposure builds the brand. All three matter - but only cash revenue can pay for operations.

→Is the Auburn AleHouse arrangement documented with a formal revenue-sharing agreement?
→How does partnership revenue currently appear in H&H's financials?
→Are there more community businesses that could come in under a similar structure?
05
Staff Development and Workshops
Training · Wellness · Team-Building · Club Teams
Proof of Concept - Scale It ▼

One staff development day at TRUSD is a proof of concept with an existing contract partner. This is the most mission-aligned earned income play on the list. Training, wellness, and leadership development extends H&H's expertise beyond programming and creates a replicable service that districts and community organizations will pay for. With TRUSD already in the relationship, the upsell path is clear. The caution is not scaling prematurely before core infrastructure is solid.

→Has the TRUSD staff development day been evaluated? What was the response?
→Is there a pricing model and curriculum framework for future offerings?
→Does current staffing support expanding this without diluting core contracts?
06
Creative Services
Photography · Video · Event Media · Social Media Packages
Future Play - Structural Review Required ▼

H&H has built what is effectively an in-house media agency inside a 501(c)(3), with real brand relationships generating real creative output. The opportunity is significant. The structure requires thought. As a nonprofit, generating revenue from activities not substantially related to the exempt purpose can trigger Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT). Whether Creative Services is mission-adjacent enough to qualify as program-related income - or whether it needs a separate structure - is a question that needs legal and strategic clarity before it scales.

Structural Flag: UBIT

When a 501(c)(3) generates income from activities not substantially related to its exempt purpose, that income may be subject to Unrelated Business Income Tax. Options include documenting mission alignment clearly, exploring a social enterprise subsidiary LLC, or separating financial tracking. A nonprofit attorney or CPA should weigh in before this stream scales.

→Is content creation revenue currently tracked separately in H&H's financials?
→Has a nonprofit attorney reviewed UBIT exposure for this stream?
→How can this be structured to protect H&H's tax-exempt status while capturing the revenue?
07
Long-Term Capital - Site Acquisition
SHRA · CDBG · LISC Sacramento · CDFIs · Surplus Property
Long-Term Play ▼

A permanent facility in Del Paso Heights is a 3-to-5-year play that changes H&H's cost structure, funder credibility, and community permanence simultaneously. Del Paso Heights' geography creates eligibility advantages most nonprofits don't have access to - CDBG funding through SHRA, CDFI financing through LISC Sacramento and Northern California Community Loan Fund, and potential access to surplus city or county property. Owning a facility reduces long-term program costs and signals to funders that H&H is built to last. The groundwork starts now even though the timeline is long.

→Has H&H explored SHRA's CDBG program for Del Paso Heights specifically?
→Is there a relationship with LISC Sacramento or N. CA Community Loan Fund?
→What does a facility actually cost in the target area and what is the realistic timeline?

Section Three

Questions to answer together.

A real strategy for H&H can only be built by the people who know the org, the community, and the mission. These are the questions that make the whiteboard conversation productive.

Question 01
What does H&H look like in three years?
Not financially. Programmatically. How many schools, how many kids, how many communities? The answer to this question sets the revenue target. Everything else in the strategy flows from here.
Question 02
What does it cost for José and Nate to lead full-time?
The working figure is $80K per founder, $160K total. This needs to be confirmed or corrected. It is the number the strategy is built around. If it's different, the timeline and sequence change.
Question 03
Which scenario is H&H willing to pursue?
Conservative, Moderate, or Ambitious - each requires different levels of capacity and attention from José and Nate. The right answer is the one H&H can actually execute without overextending the people running the mission.
Question 04
What is the Trailblazers Club target?
Using the grid above - what is the realistic donor count and average gift to aim for in Year 1? Setting a specific number creates accountability and a launch plan around a real goal, not a vague intention.
Question 05
What is the TRUSD renewal plan?
The current $105K contract expires May 2026. Protecting and growing earned income is the first move in any scenario. What does renewal look like and is there a path to expanding the scope?
Question 06
Who else needs to be in this conversation?
Building toward leadership sustainability is not just an operational decision - it's a governance and community one. Marion, Jeff, the coaching staff, and active partners may each have a role in future conversations. Who is in which conversation, and when, is part of what gets decided at the whiteboard.
At the Whiteboard
  • Define what H&H looks like in three years, programmatically
  • Confirm or adjust the full-time founder compensation figure
  • Choose a scenario pathway and set the revenue target
  • Set a specific Trailblazers Club Year 1 goal using the grid
  • Map all seven revenue streams - what is active, what gets built first, what waits
  • Define cash revenue structure for brand partnerships
  • Confirm TRUSD renewal strategy before May 2026 expiration
  • Identify the first capacity-building grant opportunities to pursue
  • Discuss who from the board, coaching staff, and partner network joins future conversations
