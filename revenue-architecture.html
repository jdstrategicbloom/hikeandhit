Hike & Hit  —  Internal Strategy Brief
H&H Revenue and Funding Architecture
Prepared for Whiteboard Planning Session
This brief grew out of our recent conversation about mapping H&H's revenue streams. It organizes what we discussed, adds context to each mechanism, surfaces the structural questions underneath, and sets the agenda for a deeper planning session. It doesn't have a plan at the end. It has the map needed to build one together.
Section One
The Mission and What It Requires
What We Are Building Toward
More kids served. More communities reached.
H&H operating at the scale its mission demands.
That doesn't happen while José and Nate are splitting their time between delivering programs and leading an organization. The mission requires builders at the top, not just deliverers in the field.
H&H is doing something real. The programs work, the relationships are strong, and the community trust has been earned. That is not the problem. The structural problem is this: H&H is currently funded to run programs, not to build an organization. Every dollar coming in is spoken for by program delivery. There is no slack for the leadership capacity, systems, or infrastructure that would allow H&H to reach more kids in more places.
The question this brief is designed to surface is not about money. It is about mission reach. How far can H&H go if José and Nate remain in their current operating position? And what becomes possible if they don't have to be?

Section Two
The Full Revenue and Funding Landscape
H&H has six distinct revenue streams either active or available. Most organizations at this stage over-rely on one or two. Understanding what each mechanism does and does not unlock is the foundation of a strategy that can actually move the mission forward at scale.
01   Contracts
Active — Protect and Grow
School and program contracts are the backbone of H&H's earned income. This is the only revenue H&H fully controls — no application cycle, no funder approval, no restricted use. The TRUSD contract at $105K is the current anchor. Growing this tier through new district relationships is the most direct path to unrestricted revenue. SCUSD and EGUSD are the next prospects.
TRUSD ($105K) ✓ NUSD ELOP ✓ CCCA Sports Academy ✓ SCUSD → EGUSD →
02   Trailblazers Club
Build and Scale
Monthly recurring giving from individual donors. Recurring giving is the most sustainable community revenue an org can build — it compounds over time, runs independent of grant cycles, and grows with storytelling and community connection. Trailblazers Club is the right vehicle. It needs a launch plan, a donor cultivation strategy, and consistency. This is a Year 1 priority.
Monthly donor program → Community perks and storytelling → Long-term sustainability base →
The Missing Layer — Highest Strategic Priority
03   Grants and Institutional Funding
Active and Expand — Shift the Mix
H&H already has an active grants pipeline. The critical shift is in what kinds of grants are being pursued. Program grants — Golden 1, Sutter, foundations — fund program delivery but cannot fund the org. Capacity-building grants and general operating support grants are the missing layer. These fund staff, infrastructure, leadership, and systems. They are specifically designed to help organizations like H&H build themselves, not just run programs. Activating this grant category is the most direct route to closing the structural gap.
Golden 1 Credit Union ✓ Sutter Health ✓ Capacity-building grants → General operating support → OJJDP (Federal) → CNRA Outdoor Equity → Sierra Health Foundation →
04   Brand Partnerships and Sponsorships
Active — Revenue Structure Needs Clarity
H&H has active brand relationships with real visibility and community reach. Auburn AleHouse is the most developed — a co-branded beer with three more releases in progress and event partnerships that extend the mission into community spaces. Old Trapper, Kühl, and Branded Bills are active partners as well. The strategic question is what the actual revenue structure looks like for each. Are these producing cash revenue, in-kind product, or exposure? The answer determines how they fit into the sustainability strategy.
Auburn AleHouse (co-branded beer) ✓ Old Trapper ✓ Kühl ✓ Branded Bills ✓
Needs definition at the whiteboard: What is the cash revenue from each partnership vs. in-kind vs. exposure? How replicable is the AleHouse model? Are there more community businesses to bring in under a similar structure?
05   Staff Development and Workshops
Proof of Concept — Scale It
One staff development day at TRUSD is a proof of concept with an existing contract partner. This is the most mission-aligned earned income play on the list. Training, wellness, team-building, and leadership development for schools and organizations extends H&H's expertise beyond programming and creates a replicable service that districts and community organizations will pay for. With TRUSD already in the relationship, the upsell path is clear.
TRUSD staff dev day ✓ (completed) School wellness and team-building → Leadership training → Club teams →
06   Creative Services
Future Play — Structural Review Required
H&H has real creative capacity — photography, video, content production, event media — that brands like Old Trapper and Kühl already recognize. The opportunity is significant. The structure requires thought. As a nonprofit, generating revenue from unrelated business activities can trigger tax implications under IRS guidelines (UBIT). Whether Creative Services is mission-adjacent enough to qualify as program-related income, or whether it requires a separate structure, is a question that needs legal and strategic clarity before it is built out as a revenue line.
Photography ○ Video production ○ Event media coverage ○ Social media content packages ○ Brand partnerships ○
Before scaling: Get clarity on nonprofit revenue rules and whether Creative Services can be structured in a way that protects H&H's tax-exempt status. This is a high-potential play that deserves the right foundation.
Section Three
Questions to Answer Together
A real strategy for H&H can only be built by the people who know the org, the community, and the mission. These are the questions that make that conversation productive.
Question 01
What does H&H look like in three years?
Not financially. Programmatically. How many schools, how many kids, how many communities? The answer to this question sets the revenue target. Everything else in the strategy flows from here.
Question 02
What does it cost for José and Nate to lead full-time?
The working figure in this brief is $80K per founder, $160K total. This needs to be confirmed or corrected. It is the number the strategy is built around. If it is different, the timeline and sequence change.
Question 03
What is the TRUSD renewal plan?
The current $105K contract expires May 2026. Protecting and growing earned income is the first move in any strategy. What does renewal look like? Is there a path to a larger contract or a staff development add-on? This shapes everything that follows.
Question 04
What is the real revenue picture on brand partnerships?
Auburn AleHouse, Old Trapper, Kühl, Branded Bills. What is cash, what is in-kind, what is exposure? The answer determines how these partnerships fit into a sustainability strategy and whether the AleHouse model can be replicated with other community businesses.
Question 05
What can realistically be prioritized in Year 1?
Trailblazers Club, capacity-building grants, staff development expansion, and partnership definition all deserve attention. Not all at once. What is H&H ready to move on without overextending the people running the mission?
Question 06
Who else needs to be in this conversation?
Building toward leadership sustainability and scaling the mission is not just an operational decision. It is a governance and community decision. The following people may need to be brought into future conversations: Marion, Jeff, the coaching staff, and active partners. Who is in which conversation, and when, is part of what gets decided at the whiteboard.

  • Define what H&H looks like in three years, programmatically

  • Define the cash revenue structure for Auburn AleHouse and brand partnerships

  • Set a Trailblazers Club launch window and define what readiness looks like
  • Confirm TRUSD renewal strategy before May 2026 expiration
  • Decide who from the board, coaching staff, and partner network joins future conversations
